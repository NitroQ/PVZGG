Maps and Shop

Crazetopher David Blazing III known commonly throughout the main series as Crazy Dave, is the deuteragonist and a “sort of” a narrator in the game. He also has a shop called Crazy Dave's Twiddydinkies that he runs from the back of his car, once the player finds his car keys (after Level 3-4). He sells several plant upgrades, extra defenses, and Zen Garden items. He is rarely seen without a pot on his head. Despite his eccentric behavior, he has repeatedly proven himself to be a highly competent inventor and engineer.Crazy Dave is based on real-life game designer and former PopCap employee David Rohrl. The sandaled feet, scruffy facial features, and even the car cluttered with board games are all nods to Rohrl's own peculiarities. Rohrl still frequents PopCap parties with a pot on his head.

Extra Seed Slots


They are unlocked in order; the next one is unlocked immediately after the last one was purchased. The seventh slot (and sometimes the eighth slot) can be unlocked before the shop is unlocked, as Crazy Dave asks the player to buy it once they get $750.

Cost:

  • Seventh seed slot: $750
  • Eighth seed slot: $5000
  • Ninth seed slot: $20,000
  • Tenth seed slot: $80,000

Extra Defenses

These items are extra lines of defense used either in conjunction or replacement of standard Lawn Mowers.

  • Pool Cleaner : $1000

    Pool Cleaners are Lawn Mowers for pool lanes. Otherwise, regular Lawn Mowers only stop zombies that are out of the pool, as they will only destroy the zombies in front of them for a short distance due to the lack of waterproofing that would have otherwise rendered the pool cleaner redundant.

  • Garden Rake: $200

    Garden Rake is a semi-reusable item sold to the player, It costs $200, lasts for three levels, and it can be bought again any time after it runs out. It is automatically placed in the lane where the first zombie will appear and the zombie that steps on it gets killed instantly.

  • Roof Cleaner: $3000

    Roof Cleaner is an item available in Plants vs. Zombies. It functions exactly like an ordinary Lawn Mower in the front yard, except that it is used on the roof. If not purchased, the player will not have any last line of defense on the roof.

Upgrades

Upgrades must be planted on top of other plants. These are unlocked in a series of two at a time. the first set is available after Level 3-4, the next is unlocked after Level 4-4, a penultimate duo after Level 5-1, and a final two after the end of Adventure Mode.

After Level 3-4


  • Gatling Pea - $5000/$53000*

    An upgrade for the Repeater that shoots four peas per round.

  • Twin Sunflower - $5000/$51000*

    An upgrade for the Sunflower that gives twice as much sun.


After Level 4-4


  • Gloom-shroom - $7500/$25000*

    An upgrade for the Fume-shroom that shoots in eight directions at once with significantly reduced range.

  • Cattail - $10,000/$51000*

    An upgrade for the Lily Pad that can target any zombie on the screen. They can also pop Balloons.


After Level 5-1


  • Spikerock - $7500/$30000*

    An upgrade for the Spikeweed that deals double damage and can withstand nine Gargantuar smashes/popped tires.

  • Gold Magnet - $3000/$12000*

    An upgrade for the Magnet-shroom that collects money for the player instead of Metalic Objects.


After Completing Adventure Mode


  • Winter Melon - $10,000/$128000*

    An upgrade for the Melon-pult that slows targets in addition to splash damages.

  • Cob Cannon - $20,000/$158000*

    An upgrade for the Kernel-pult that launches explosive corn cobs at zombies, these have to be placed in a 1x2 area to be able to upgrade.
